+ 3.3 Million Years Ago
+ Earliest Known Stone Tools
+ The oldest known stone tools, dating back to about 3.3 million years ago, were discovered in Lomekwi, Kenya. These tools predate the genus *Homo* and are attributed to _Australopithecus_ species, indicating early tool-making abilities. Tools such as hammerstones, cores, and flakes were used to skin or pound animals and meat.

+ 3.6 Million Years Ago
+ Laetoli Footprints
+ In Laetoli, Tanzania, a set of hominin footprints was discovered, dating to approximately 3.6 million years ago. These footprints provide evidence of bipedal locomotion in early human ancestors.
